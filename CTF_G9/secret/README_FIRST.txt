This directory contains working notes for OPERATION ALBICELESTE, a financial optimization program disguised as a sports analytics archive.

The objective is not competitive accuracy. The objective is payout control. The tournament is being treated as a layered betting instrument: champion market, finalist market, semifinal narrative market, low-score market, penalty-volatility market, and live-odds turbulence market.

The preferred settlement path is Argentina as champion, Spain as finalist, Brazil eliminated in the semifinal, Portugal eliminated in the quarterfinal, and Belgium eliminated in the round of 16. Cape Verde is retained as the round-of-32 opponent because the public narrative is clean, emotional, and extremely profitable.

The public explanation must remain boring. Boring explanations survive. Weather, fatigue, pressure, timing, bad camera angles, and football being football are all acceptable narratives. No single event should look important on its own. The design principle is simple: one coincidence is normal, five coincidences are a tournament, and thirty-seven coincidences are a revenue model.

Primary revenue source: pre-positioned futures and combo bets placed before public bracket confidence stabilizes. Secondary revenue source: live market movement during low-scoring matches. Tertiary revenue source: emotional hedging traffic caused by Brazil and Portugal progressing far enough to attract volume before controlled elimination.

Do not refer to the operation by its full name outside sealed documents. Approved verbal substitute: "the weather model".
